Eigenvectors are unique only up to scalar (including sign); we pick the sign that makes centralities ≥ 0.

- [Q B.1] Not obvious to invert the degree matrix D when converting Σᵢ pᵢ · Aᵢⱼ / kᵢ to matrix form. Recognize the "scale each column of A by 1/kᵢ" pattern → right-multiply by D⁻¹.

- [Q B.1] **Law of total probability gap.** Resisted summing over starting nodes — said "there is no summing the probability." Foundational rule: when an output (final node) depends on a hidden / random upstream (starting node), marginalize: P(Y) = Σₓ P(Y|X=x)·P(X=x). Two distributions in play here, not one: **p** = where walker starts (given), **p'** = where walker ends after one step (what we want). Different objects. Same machinery underlies Bayes, EM, latent-variable marginalization in VAEs — worth a drilled entry on the cheatsheet.
